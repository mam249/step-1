Blog

Most people probably don't know what waacking, locking and house are. Well, these 3 things are my escape from all the routine work I get from everyday life. These 3 things are dance styles that I can't live without. All of them are very different, but I just happen to mix them in my freestyle all the time.

Read the next 3 posts to understand what they are.

Waacking was originated in the 1970s on the west coast in the US. Waacking was founded by the LGBTQ+ community to express their feelings and emotions. Waacking music is usually disco and funk with vocals. As for the moves, waackers, people who dance waacking, use their arms and hands in a very fast pace to demonstrate the melody.

Locking came to life in the 1960s in the US when its founder, Don Campbell, accidentally created a new move from a hip hop move "Robot Shuffle". Like waacking, locking is danced to funky and happy music. Lockers can be recognised because of their "locking" moves. These moves originate from a concept of freezing from a fast movement and "locking" in a position by holding it for a short period of time which is then followed by their fast moves.

House dance was founded around 1980s in the night clubs of Chicago and New York. This was the time when house music started becoming more popular, so people created their own way to dance to it. House mostly involves very fast paced footwork, smooth body waves and floor work. It was also heavily influenced by salsa and other latin dances. House dancers use steps in their routine and create their own variations of foundational moves.
